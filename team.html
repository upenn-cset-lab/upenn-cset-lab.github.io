Principal Investigator

Kyle R. Jackson, MD, PhD
Principal Investigator Assistant Professor of Surgery
Assistant Professor of Biostatistics and Epidemiology
Penn Medicine

Dr. Jackson is a clinically active transplant surgeon and epidemiologist whose research integrates clinical medicine with population science to investigate how climate change, environmental exposures, and place-based factors influence solid organ transplant outcomes. His work leverages national transplant registries and large-scale healthcare datasets to address risk stratification, equity, and environmental determinants of transplant success.

His work focuses on transplant exposome science, integrating national clinical datasets with high-resolution environmental exposure surfaces to investigate how climate and contextual factors influence transplant outcomes.

He previously conducted federally supported epidemiologic research at Arbor Research, contributing to multi-center studies using national transplant registries.
